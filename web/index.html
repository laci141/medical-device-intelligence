About this tool

For everyone

This page shows how much public-record attention a device gets — reports filed, recalls issued, studies run. High attention usually tracks how widely a device is used and how well it is documented. It is never a safety verdict, and the absence of records is not evidence of safety.

For researchers

Sources: openFDA (device/event MAUDE, enforcement, UDI), ClinicalTrials.gov v2, PubMed — all keyless public APIs, queried live with server-side totals. Every signal card carries its formula and sources under “Methodology”, and confidence levels are stated per reading.

Limits

No usage denominator: public data does not include units sold, so volumes are NOT failure rates. MAUDE has a publication lag (the newest weeks look artificially quiet), many reports lack event dates, and device name matching is textual — subsidiaries, brand names, and procedure-like names can blur the picture. Signal weights and thresholds are editorial choices, documented per card under “Methodology”. Data-quality readings on this page quantify some of these gaps.
